# Renewal Workflow

The Renewal Workflow is what runs on `/contracts/:id` once a contract enters its evaluation window: the AI renewal brief, the 6-stage checklist, the negotiation log, and the savings tracker.

## What you'll see

On the contract detail page, three cards focus on the renewal:

**Renewal & Savings card** — original ask, final negotiated price, computed savings delta. This is what feeds the dashboard YTD savings number.

**Renewal Workflow card** — the 6-stage progress bar, shown as a 3×2 grid of numbered steps. Surfaces when the contract is `under_review` OR within 90 days of `endDate`. Each step records who marked it and when.

**Negotiation Log card** — a free-form textarea backed by `Contract.negotiationLog`. Where you record back-and-forth, leverage points used, concessions made.

When AI is enabled, a fourth card appears: **AI Renewal Brief** — a 4-section structured summary of the contract for the negotiator.

## The 6-stage checklist

1. **Renewal notice sent to vendor** — you've notified the vendor of intent to review, or opened the cancel window (sometimes implicit; many SaaS skip this).
2. **Usage reviewed / quote requested** — reviewed seat/license utilization and requested a renewal quote.
3. **Proposal received** — vendor's renewal quote or proposal arrived.
4. **Under negotiation** — actively negotiating terms, price, or scope.
5. **Approved & awaiting signature** — final agreement reached internally; pending signatures.
6. **Signed / renewed** — done. Set the new end date, optionally chain via `parentContractId`.

When a contract first enters `under_review` status, `evaluationStartedById` and `evaluationStartedAt` auto-populate from the user who flipped it. Useful for "who's driving this renewal" questions.

## The AI renewal brief

Click "Generate Brief" on the contract detail. The AI reads the contract record + recent vendor news + the negotiation log and produces a four-section structured summary:

- **Situation** — what this contract is, what's at stake, the headline numbers and dates
- **Market** — outside signal: vendor news, comparable pricing, competitive context (web-search-enriched when configured)
- **Tactics** — your negotiation angles based on utilization, alternatives, leverage points
- **Watch For** — auto-renewal clauses, escalators, minimum commits, notice requirements, and anything else that could bite

Optional sections (License Utilization, Co-Term Opportunities, Quote-Request Hygiene, Recommended Strategy, Internal Stakeholder Map) render below the four when an admin enables them in Settings → AI → Renewal Brief Sections.

The brief is cached on the contract record. Click "Refresh" to regenerate — but each refresh costs an AI call. Refresh when something material changed: new utilization data, an auto-renewal clause discovered in re-reading, a vendor news item that changed the picture.

## Savings tracking

Two fields drive this: `originalAsk` (the vendor's first quote at renewal) and `finalNegotiatedPrice` (what you actually signed for). The delta is the savings. It rolls up into the dashboard's YTD Savings tile.

The Savings Ledger report at `/reports/savings-ledger` is the full audit trail: every contract with one or both fields set, with delta, savings %, and category rollups.

## Common workflows

**"I'm about to call the vendor — what do I need?"** Open the AI brief. Skim Watch For (so you don't get surprised by a clause you forgot). Skim Tactics (so you have something to push on). Have the Negotiation Log open in another tab to record what gets said.

**"How do I track that I countered with a lower price?"** Negotiation Log → add a line with the date, the price you offered, the rationale, and the vendor's response. Free-form text; this becomes the historical record.

**"The vendor came back lower — how do I update savings?"** Renewal & Savings card → set `finalNegotiatedPrice` to the new number. Dashboard YTD updates immediately.

**"This contract just signed — what's my final step?"** Renewal Checklist → mark Signed. Set new `endDate`. Optionally create a successor contract and set its `parentContractId` to this one's id so the chain is visible.

## When something looks wrong

**The AI brief is missing the leverage I expected.** Two causes: (1) Utilization data isn't filled in — `seatsLicensed` and `seatsActivelyInUse` are what tells the brief you're over-licensed. (2) Vendor news for this vendor is sparse. Add watch terms via the news scanner to capture more signal.

**The checklist isn't progressing — I marked Notice Sent but it didn't save.** Refresh the page. If still not saving, check the activity log for the contract — write events show there. If nothing's there, you may be in demo mode (writes blocked) or a viewer/consultant role.

**The savings number on the dashboard doesn't match the Savings Ledger.** The dashboard rounds to whole dollars and only counts contracts where BOTH `originalAsk` and `finalNegotiatedPrice` are set. The Savings Ledger is more lenient — it shows partial data too. The dashboard number under-reports when your team isn't filling in both fields consistently.
