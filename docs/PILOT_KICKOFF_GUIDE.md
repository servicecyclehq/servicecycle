# Pilot Kickoff Guide

This guide covers what happens in the first two weeks of your pilot. No technical background required — if you can send an email and open a PDF, you can complete day 1 in under an hour.

---

## Day 1 — Account Setup (~45 minutes)

### What you'll need

- Your email address and a password
- 2–3 existing test reports as PDFs from one customer site (any format: PowerDB, Megger, Word-based, etc.)
- The name of one customer site to start with

### Step 1 — Log in and set up your account

1. Open the invitation email from ServiceCycle and click the link to set your password.
2. On first login you'll land on the Fleet Dashboard — your view across all customer sites.
3. Click your company name in the top right and confirm your contact details.

### Step 2 — Create your first site

1. Click **New Site** in the top navigation.
2. Enter the customer site name, address, and type (industrial, commercial, utility, etc.).
3. Don't worry about filling every field now — you can edit later.

### Step 3 — Upload your first test reports

1. Navigate to the site you just created and click **Import Reports**.
2. Drag in 2–3 PDF test reports from that site.
3. ServiceCycle reads the PDFs and extracts equipment records, test results, and deficiencies. This typically takes 1–3 minutes per report.
4. You'll see a review screen showing what was extracted. Spot-check 2–3 records against the original PDF before confirming.

**End of Day 1:** You should see equipment records in the asset list, any open deficiencies flagged, and at least one active maintenance schedule in place.

---

## Week 1 — Review Extracted Data and Invite Your Team (~2–3 hours across the week)

### Review extracted data

Open the asset list for your site. For each piece of equipment, check:

- Equipment type (transformer, switchgear, relay, etc.)
- Manufacturer and model
- Condition rating (C1 / C2 / C3 — per NFPA 70B)
- Whether the maintenance schedule interval matches your standard practice

Open the **Deficiencies** tab. Items marked CRITICAL or HIGH were extracted from the test report narrative or measured values. Confirm these match what's in the PDF.

### Fix mismatches

- If a piece of equipment was misidentified, correct the equipment type — the maintenance schedule will update automatically.
- If a deficiency was missed, add it manually from the asset record.
- If the schedule interval is wrong for your practice, edit it directly on the schedule record.

### Invite your field tech

1. Go to **Settings → Team** and click **Invite**.
2. Enter the field tech's email and select the **Field Tech** role.
3. They'll receive an invitation email with access to the mobile-friendly field view.

**By end of Week 1:** Equipment records are verified, deficiencies are confirmed or corrected, and your field tech has access.

---

## Week 2 — First Outage Plan and Leave-Behind (~1–2 hours)

### Generate your first outage plan

1. Navigate to the site's **Work Orders** tab and click **New Work Order**.
2. ServiceCycle shows you the open deficiencies and upcoming maintenance due for that site, sorted by priority.
3. Select the items you'd address in an outage visit and proceed.
4. The work order shows applicable NFPA 70B tasks and any special requirements (arc-flash study currency, PPE category).

### Generate your first customer leave-behind

1. From the site overview, click **Generate Leave-Behind**.
2. Select what to include: equipment summary, deficiency list, maintenance schedule, arc-flash status.
3. Download the PDF. This is what you'd hand to the customer at the end of a site visit.

**By end of Week 2:** You've completed one full cycle — report in, data extracted, work order out, leave-behind generated. The platform is ready for a second site.

---

## What to bring to the week-2 check-in

- One or two questions about the workflow that didn't fit how your team works
- One piece of equipment or test type that didn't extract correctly (we want to know about these)
- Whether the leave-behind format matches what you currently give customers, or what's different
