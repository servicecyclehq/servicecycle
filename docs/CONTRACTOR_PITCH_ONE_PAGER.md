# ServiceCycle for Electrical Contractors

**Turn every test report into a live maintenance program — automatically.**

---

## The Problem

After every site visit, your technicians write thorough test reports. Those reports identify equipment condition, measured values, and deficiencies — exactly the information a customer needs to maintain their electrical infrastructure. But once the report is delivered, that data sits in a PDF. Deficiencies go unremediated for months or years not because customers don't care, but because no one surfaces them between outage windows. Compliance is managed by memory and spreadsheet. When an auditor or an insurer asks about the maintenance program, the answer is: "It's in the reports somewhere."

The contractor does the work, writes the report, and the value of that data disappears.

---

## What ServiceCycle Does

ServiceCycle reads your existing test reports — PowerDB, Megger, Word-based, any format — and builds a live equipment record for every asset in your customer's facility. Each record carries an NFPA 70B maintenance schedule with the correct interval for that equipment type and condition rating. Open deficiencies stay visible and linked to the work order that resolves them. Arc-flash label data is stored and tracked against study expiry. When you're ready for a site visit, the system shows you exactly what's due, what's open, and what PPE is required. When you're done, it generates the customer leave-behind. Your technicians don't change how they test — they upload the report they already wrote, and ServiceCycle does the rest.

---

## The ROI

The work ServiceCycle surfaces already exists in your test reports — it just isn't being systematically followed up on. Consider a shop managing 20 accounts with an average of 15 major assets per site: that's 300 assets. If 30% have an open deficiency at any given time, that's 90 open items. If 40% of those convert to a quote request in a given year, that's 36 additional service calls the shop surfaces through systematic follow-up rather than customer initiative. At your average service ticket value, the math is straightforward. The platform doesn't create new work — it captures the work you already found and makes sure it gets quoted.
