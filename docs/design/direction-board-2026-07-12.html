Direction B

Control Room

Effort: ~3–5 agent sessions (dashboard restructure; other pages get A)Layout risk: dashboard onlyBest for: the 10-second demo impression

The dark chrome stops at the sidebar today. Direction B extends it into an instrument band — the four numbers that decide "are we in trouble?" live on ink, in mono, like a test set's readout. Below, the light zone splits into evidence (left) and a sticky action queue (right) so "what do I do next?" is always on screen. This is the layout your content has been asking for: verdict → proof → action.

Direction C

Field Report

Effort: 1–2 weeks, incremental (re-template core pages)Layout risk: highestBest for: unmistakable identity

Your users live in NETA test reports, PE-stamped studies, and inspection PDFs. Direction C makes the app read like the documents this industry already trusts: a masthead instead of a greeting, numbered sections, double-rules, big light-weight mono figures, hairline-ruled tables — almost no boxes at all. Navigation collapses to an ink icon rail. Nothing on the market looks like this; nothing AI-generated does either.
