# ServiceCycle — Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy

**Owner:** Engineering / Operations  
**Effective:** 2026-06-25  
**Review cadence:** Annually or after a material incident

---

## Commitment

Everyone who contributes to ServiceCycle — employees, contractors, and vendors
with repository or data access — is expected to act with integrity, honesty, and
in the best interests of customers and users.

---

## Core Principles

**Data integrity** — Never alter, falsify, or suppress audit records, activity
logs, or compliance data. The audit trail exists to protect customers; tampering
with it is a termination-level offense.

**Confidentiality** — Customer data, API keys, environment secrets, and
unreleased product plans are confidential. Do not share them with unauthorized
parties or use them for personal gain.

**Conflicts of interest** — Disclose any financial or personal relationship with
a vendor, competitor, or customer that could influence a business decision. When
in doubt, disclose and recuse.

**Access least-privilege** — Request only the permissions you need. Do not
access systems, data, or administrative functions beyond the scope of your
current task.

**Respectful collaboration** — Treat all colleagues, customers, and community
members with respect. Discrimination, harassment, and retaliation are not
tolerated.

**Security-first** — Report security vulnerabilities or suspected data
incidents immediately (see `docs/INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md`). Never delay or
conceal a potential breach.

**Compliance** — Follow applicable laws and regulations (GDPR, state breach
notification statutes, NFPA/OSHA where arc-flash data is involved). When
uncertain, ask before acting.

---

## Reporting

Violations or concerns should be reported to the company owner (via direct
message or at the security contact listed in `/.well-known/security.txt`).
Reports are treated confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports a
concern in good faith is itself a violation of this policy.

---

## Consequences

Violations may result in removal of access, termination of engagement, or
referral to appropriate legal authorities depending on severity.

---

## Acknowledgement

All individuals with production or repository access are expected to have read
and understood this policy. The Git commit history serves as an implicit
acknowledgement — access to the repository requires accepting the repository
host's terms, and this document is part of the project.
