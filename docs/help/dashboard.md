# Dashboard

The Dashboard is the morning-coffee view of your maintenance program: what needs
attention today, what's coming, and where the open risk sits. It answers one
question first — *what do I need to do?* — and leaves the detail to the pages it
links into.

## What you'll see

**Summary tiles** across the top: how many assets you're tracking, how many
maintenance tasks are current versus overdue, open deficiencies by severity, and
your overall compliance posture.

**Due and overdue** — the tasks that are past due or coming due soon, pulled from
the compliance calendar. This is the panel to read first; overdue work is the
fastest path to falling out of compliance.

**Open deficiencies by severity** — immediate, recommended, and advisory findings
across all your equipment, so the things a technician flagged don't sit unseen.

**The pipeline ahead** — a forward look at maintenance coming due over the next
weeks and months, so booking and outage planning aren't last-minute.

## How the data lands here

Every tile pulls live from the assets, schedules, and deficiencies you have access
to. Viewers scoped to specific sites see only those sites, and the totals reflect
that scope. Next-due dates are computed from each asset's condition and last
completion, so the numbers move on their own as work is recorded and conditions
change.

## Common workflows

**"What do I need to do today?"** Read the due/overdue panel first — anything
overdue is the priority.

**"Where's our biggest risk?"** The deficiencies-by-severity tile; immediate
findings are the ones to clear first.

**"What's coming so I can plan an outage?"** The pipeline view, then the Outage
Planner to group the de-energized work into a shutdown window.

## When something looks wrong

**The overall compliance number looks low.** Uncovered assets — equipment with no
maintenance tasks applied yet — weigh against the score on purpose, so applying
the right tasks both raises coverage and improves the number. See *Scores,
Ratings & Forecasts* for exactly how the figure is built.

**An overdue item I just completed is still showing.** The dashboard reflects
recorded completions; make sure the work order or schedule completion was actually
saved, then reload.

**A site's numbers look incomplete.** If you're a scope-restricted viewer, the
dashboard only counts the sites you're assigned to — that's expected.
